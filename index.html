Me

I am currently working towards a Bachelors of Science in Computer Engineering at the University of Pittsburgh's Swanson School of Engineering (expected to graduate in August 2023). So far, I've kept a 3.99 GPA and earned the First Year Success Award, a spot on Dean's List, and Honors Academic Standing.

Through coursework and internships, I've learned to use C++, C (including unit testing using C frameworks), Python, ARM-Cortex-M MCUs (ST, NXP), LabVIEW, MATLAB, Linux, STM32CubeIDE, Git, Confluence, and Jira. I also have experience in CAD software like Altium Designer, Fusion 360, LTSpice, and fabrication techniques such as 3D Printing, soldering, and populating PCBs using a stencil. I've also picked up some automation skills such as PLC and HMI programming, working with 6 axis robot arms, and using machine vision (Cognex).

Currently, I'm back at school (who would have thought this day would come) after taking 16 months off to intern for various companies. I am also working part time at an automation startup, Loupe. More details to come soon on what I have been working on there! You can also check out some of the other places I've worked in the past here.

Outside of school and work, I enjoy hanging out with friends, reading, playing video games, trying new restaurants and food, going to the gym, and working on cool side projects.

    Projects

    Futori Six-Axis Robot Arm. I'm working on a 3D printed, mini-industrial robot arm with gearboxes, stepper motors, bearings, and belt drives all incorporated into the build. I soldered and wired thumb joysticks onto a protoboard to control the robot's motion and programmed it using C++ on an Arduino. I publish progress updates to over 1300 followers on an instagram account (@electronics_garage) dedicated to my builds. I eventually founded a community of makers that are pursuing a similar project, where we often bounce ideas off of each other and talk lots of robotics. I really encourage you to visit the project gallery here!

    TartanHacks Hackathon. At Carnegie Mellon University in 2019, my team was awarded the ScottyLab’s Grand Prize for designing and building a text-to-braille converter in under 24 hours. You can check more out about here.

    Interview Prep Guide. Created an interview guide covering behavioral questions all the way to advanced technical questions. Topics focused on PCB design, basic electrical engineering questions, coding questions, embedded systems questions, and more. Included in the guide are 70+ real interview questions and a 40+ page answer key! Please contact me if you would like to request a copy.
